## How to Use These Templates

Each template is optimized for a specific audience with appropriate language, emphasis, and structure. Choose the template that matches who you're presenting to:

- **Investors**: Focus on market size, returns, defensibility, scalability
- **Customers/Users**: Focus on benefits, ease of use, value gained
- **Technical Teams**: Focus on feasibility, architecture, implementation
- **Executives**: Focus on strategy, risk, resources, alignment

**Maximum length**: 2 pages per NABC document
**Style**: Professional but conversational, not academic
**Format**: Clear sections, quantified claims, scannable structure

## Tips for Using These Templates

### Before Filling Out Templates:

1. **Do your homework**:
   - Interview customers to validate the need
   - Quantify everything with real data
   - Research competitors thoroughly
   - Test your key assumptions

2. **Engage your virtual board**:
   - Present your rough idea first
   - Get feedback on each NABC element
   - Iterate based on their challenges
   - Then fill out the template

3. **Tailor to audience**:
   - What does THIS specific audience care about?
   - What language do they speak?
   - What level of detail do they need?

### While Filling Out Templates:

1. **Be specific, not vague**:

   - Include customer quotes
   - Reference pilot data
   - Provide concrete examples
   - Use comparisons

3. **Quantify the value ratio**:
   - Always aim for 2-10x better
   - Be specific about which dimensions
   - Compare apples to apples

### After First Draft:

1. **Test with your virtual board**:
   - "v_Warren, is the competitive moat clear?"
   - "v_Steve, is this simple enough?"
   - "v_Ben, would you invest based on this?"

2. **Iterate ruthlessly**:
   - First draft is never final
   - Expect 3-5 iterations minimum
   - Each cycle strengthens it

3. **Practice the pitch**:
   - Can you deliver this in 5 minutes?
   - Can you answer tough questions?
   - Does it flow naturally?
