# Quick Start Guide
## NABC Innovation Lab — Setup and First Session

**Estimated time**: 15 minutes to your first working session

---

## What you need

- A Claude account at claude.ai — free tier works, Pro is better for longer sessions
- This repo downloaded to your computer
- A real idea or problem you want to work through

---

## Step 1 — Download this repo

**Option A — Download as ZIP (easiest)**
1. Go to the repo page on GitHub
2. Click the green **Code** button
3. Select **Download ZIP**
4. Unzip it somewhere you can find it

**Option B — Clone with Git**
```
git clone [repo-url]
```

You should end up with a folder containing seven files including this one.

---

## Step 2 — Create a Claude account

Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and sign up. Free tier works for getting started. If you plan to use this regularly, Pro ($20/month) gives you longer sessions and more capacity — worth it.

---

## Step 3 — Create a new Claude Project

Projects are how Claude maintains context across sessions. This is what makes the Innovation Lab work — Claude remembers your board, your frameworks, and your instructions every time you open a new conversation.

1. Log in at [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. In the left sidebar, click **Projects** then **+ New Project**
3. Name it something like "Innovation Lab" or "Strategy Board"
4. You will see fields for **Project Instructions** and **Knowledge files**

---

## Step 4 — Add the Project Instructions

This is the critical step. These instructions tell Claude how to behave in every session.

1. Open `project-instructions-v3.0.md` in any text editor
2. Select all (Ctrl+A / Cmd+A) and copy
3. Paste into the **Project Instructions** field in Claude
4. Save

Done. Claude now has its operating framework for every conversation in this project.

---

## Step 5 — Upload the knowledge files

1. In your Claude Project, find **Add content** or the upload icon
2. Upload these four files:
   - `board-member-personas.md`
   - `disciplined-entrepreneurship-framework.md`
   - `nabc-value-proposition-model-and-usage.md`
   - `nabc-templates.md`
3. Let each file finish before adding the next

You do not need to upload `README.md` or this guide — those are for you.

---

## Step 6 — Verify it works

Start a new conversation inside your project and type:

> "Introduce yourself and tell me which board members are available."

If it works, Claude will respond as the Innovation Lab facilitator, name the board, and be ready to go. If it responds generically, check that the Project Instructions were saved correctly in Step 4.

---

## Step 7 — Your first real session

Bring your actual idea. Here are four entry points:

**Rough idea, early stage**
> "I'm exploring [two or three sentences]. Haven't validated much. Let's start with a broad board discussion and figure out where I am in the DE framework."

**Clearer concept, want it challenged**
> "Here's my current value proposition: [describe it]. Challenge this ruthlessly. What are the fatal flaws and what am I assuming without evidence?"

**Working through a specific step**
> "I want to work through beachhead selection. I have these potential segments: [list them]. Let's apply the DE framework and pick one."

**Need a document fast**
> "Create an NABC for this idea: [describe it]. Target audience: [investors / customers / management]."

---

## Getting the most out of it

**Push back.** If a board member says something you disagree with, say so. The best sessions have friction. This is not a presentation tool — it's a thinking environment.

**Be specific.** "I want to build something in AI for healthcare" gets you generic questions. "I want to help primary care physicians reduce documentation time" gets you a real board discussion.

**Ask for numbers.** If a board member makes a claim without quantifying it, ask for the number. That is exactly what DE demands, and the environment is designed to support it.

**Invoke specific board members.** "v_Ben, give me your brutal reality check on the business model." "v_Warren, does this have a defensible moat?" More targeted, sharper response.

**Iterate.** Your first NABC will not be your last. Plan two or three rounds before you have something you would show someone.

---

## When you get stuck

**Claude seems to have forgotten the context:** You are probably in a new conversation outside the project. Always start from inside the project — click the project name first, then open a new chat.

**Responses feel generic:** Add specificity. Include your industry, your target customer, your constraints, and what you already know.

**The board is not challenging you enough:** Ask for it directly. "I want brutal honesty. What would kill this idea?"

**You need a specific deliverable:** Be explicit about the format. "Produce a two-page NABC document for investors, with clear sections."
